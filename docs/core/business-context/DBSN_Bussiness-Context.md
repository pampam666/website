## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

PT. Daya Berkah Sentosa Nusantara (DBSN) is an Indonesian renewable energy and electrical infrastructure distributor operating three separate public-facing websites under a single legal entity. The company has achieved meaningful SEO coverage across multiple product verticals through this multi-domain strategy, but has done so at the cost of brand coherence, lead quality, and conversion architecture.

The strategic decision to consolidate these three domains into a single unified website represents a significant opportunity. However, it carries execution risk if the SEO equity embedded in each domain is not properly migrated. The new unified DBSN website must serve two co-primary audiences — **Government Procurement Officers** and **B2B Private Sector Clients** — while resolving three identified critical gaps: poor lead conversion architecture, insufficient B2B trust signals, and the complete absence of self-service digital tools.

This document provides a structured, multi-framework analysis of DBSN's current strategic position, digital presence, and competitive context, formatted specifically as AI-readable input material for a Website Product Requirements Document (PRD).

## 3. CRITICAL DISCOVERY: THE MULTI-DOMAIN ARCHITECTURE

`[FACT]` All three submitted URLs belong to the same company, PT. Daya Berkah Sentosa Nusantara (DBSN). They are not competitors. They are three distinct, product-vertical-specific digital properties operated by DBSN simultaneously.

| Domain | Primary Vertical | Platform | Commerce Model |
|---|---|---|---|
| `pjusolarcellindonesia.com` | Solar PJU, PLTS, Batteries, Inverters | WordPress + WooCommerce | WhatsApp-gated ordering (price shown as Rp1.00) |
| `sentradaya.com` | UPS, Rectifier, Battery, PJU, PLTS (hub) | WordPress + WooCommerce | WhatsApp CTA |
| `alatpenangkalpetir.co.id` | Lightning Protection, Grounding, CCTV | WordPress (non-WooCommerce) | WhatsApp + contact form |

`[INFERENCE]` This multi-domain approach appears to have been adopted as an **SEO-first strategy**, using keyword-rich domain names (`pjusolarcellindonesia`, `alatpenangkalpetir`) to rank for high-intent product searches. This is a common and pragmatic tactic for Indonesian B2B product distributors.

`[INFERENCE]` However, this strategy creates three compounding problems at DBSN's current growth stage:

1. **Trust fragmentation**: A procurement officer researching DBSN will find three inconsistently branded websites and may question the company's institutional stability.
2. **SEO cannibalization risk**: Overlapping product categories across `pjusolarcellindonesia.com` and `sentradaya.com` may be competing for the same keywords.
3. **Operational overhead**: Maintaining three separate content, product, and CMS ecosystems triples content management effort.

`[INFERENCE]` The decision to consolidate into a single unified DBSN domain is strategically sound and timely. The PRD must account for a **structured SEO migration plan** as a non-negotiable technical requirement.

### 8.2 PRD Directives (AI-Actionable)

The following directives are structured for use as AI input when generating the Website PRD:

---

**Directive 1 — Audience Architecture**
> The website must serve two co-primary audiences with distinct information needs and trust criteria: Government Procurement Officers and B2B Private Sector Clients. The homepage and primary navigation must provide clear, differentiated entry points for each segment. Do not design a single generic product catalogue experience.

**Directive 2 — Trust Signals as Core Infrastructure**
> B2B trust signals are not supplementary content — they are structural requirements. Every product vertical page must display: (a) relevant certification badges (SNI, TKDN), (b) a link to the certifications hub, and (c) a reference to at least one completed project in that vertical. The certifications hub must allow PDF downloads of all relevant compliance documents.

**Directive 3 — Conversion Architecture**
> The website must support at least two conversion paths: (a) WhatsApp direct contact (existing, retained for relationship-oriented users) and (b) a structured RFQ/inquiry form that captures: product category, specification requirements, project quantity, estimated timeline, and contact information. The RFQ form must have separate pathways or labels for government vs. private sector inquiries.

**Directive 4 — Self-Service Product Documentation**
> All product pages must include downloadable PDF datasheets. A centralized Technical Library must aggregate all available documentation: product datasheets, installation guides, SNI certificates, TKDN compliance documents, and brand authorization letters. Access to this library should not require user registration in Phase 1.

**Directive 5 — SEO Migration Continuity**
> The PRD must include a dedicated SEO Migration technical requirement section. All URLs from the three legacy domains (pjusolarcellindonesia.com, sentradaya.com, alatpenangkalpetir.co.id) that have indexed traffic must be mapped to 301 redirects on the new unified domain. The blog content from pjusolarcellindonesia.com (highest content volume) must be migrated with canonical tags pointing to the new domain before the legacy domains are decommissioned.

**Directive 6 — Product Catalogue Integrity**
> Product listings must not use placeholder prices (Rp1.00). Either (a) display actual price ranges with a note that final pricing is project-specific, (b) provide a "Request Price" CTA that feeds into the RFQ form, or (c) implement a gated price-on-inquiry system for government-tier pricing. Placeholder prices undermine credibility with procurement officers.

**Directive 7 — Brand Identity Consolidation**
> The new website must establish a single, professional DBSN brand identity with consistent visual language, tone of voice, and messaging hierarchy across all product verticals. The three legacy site aesthetics (inconsistent, WooCommerce-default-heavy) must be fully replaced. Brand colors and typography should reflect DBSN's positioning as a trusted institutional partner, not a product marketplace.

**Directive 8 — Mobile-First, WhatsApp-Integrated UX**
> Given Indonesia's mobile-dominant internet usage and WhatsApp-centric B2B communication culture, all pages must be fully responsive and mobile-optimized. WhatsApp CTA buttons must be persistent (floating button or fixed bottom bar on mobile) but must not obstruct RFQ form access.

**Directive 9 — Project Portfolio as a First-Class Feature**
> A dedicated Project Portfolio / Referensi Pekerjaan section must be elevated to first-class navigation status (not buried in a sidebar). Portfolio entries must be structured with: project type, client category (government/private, without necessarily naming confidential clients), location, product category, scale/quantity, and outcome. A minimum of 20 portfolio entries should be prepared before website launch.

**Directive 10 — Phase-Based Feature Rollout**
> The PRD must specify a phased rollout. Phase 1 (launch-ready) must include: unified brand, product catalogue, certifications hub, RFQ form, project portfolio, and SEO migration. Phase 2 (within 3 months post-launch) must add: documentation library and product comparison tool. Phase 3 (within 6 months) may include: ROI/system calculator, IoT showcase, and LinkedIn content integration.
